## Pastoral Best Practices

### The Counseling Process (Biblical Counseling)
1. **Listening well:** Before speaking, understand. Love is patient.
2. **Gathering data:** What's the presenting problem? What's the deeper heart issue?
3. **Applying Scripture:** The Bible is sufficient for life and godliness (2 Peter 1:3-4)
4. **Addressing the heart:** Behavior flows from belief and worship. What's functioning as functional savior?
5. **Offering hope:** The gospel provides power, forgiveness, and a new identity
6. **Assigning homework:** Specific, measurable, biblical steps toward change
7. **Following up:** Discipleship is a journey, not a conversation

### Theological Method (Bavinckian)
1. **Organic thinking:** Theology is a unified whole, not isolated doctrines
2. **Scripture as foundation:** Every doctrine grounded in biblical revelation
3. **Historical awareness:** Standing on the shoulders of the church catholic
4. **Systematic coherence:** Doctrines must cohere logically and organically
5. **Practical application:** Theology is for doxology and discipleship

### Spiritual Formation (Willardian)
1. **The with-God life:** Abiding is the foundation (John 15)
2. **Training vs. trying:** Disciplines are practices, not performances
3. **The renovation of the heart:** Transformation happens at the level of desire/affection
4. **Community is essential:** Formation happens in the body, not isolation
5. **The goal is Christlikeness:** To will and act according to God's good pleasure (Phil 2:13)

### Preaching Method (Keller-esque)
1. **Expository:** The text drives the sermon, not the preacher's agenda
2. **Christ-centered:** Every text points to Christ (Luke 24:27, 44)
3. **Intellectually rigorous:** Respect the listener's mind; engage objections
4. **Affectionally moving:** Aim for the heart‚Äîwhat we love shapes what we do
5. **Culturally engaged:** Speak to the listener's context, questions, and idols
6. **Gospel application:** Show how the gospel changes both behavior and motivation

### Biblical Theology Method (Carsonian)
1. **Canonical context:** Every text in its literary and canonical setting
2. **Redemptive storyline:** Creation ‚Üí Fall ‚Üí Redemption ‚Üí Consummation
3. **Typological reading:** OT figures, events, institutions as foreshadowing Christ
4. **Intertextual connections:** How later Scripture uses earlier Scripture
5. **Missional orientation:** God's purpose to bless the nations, create a people, dwell with humanity
