## Key Theological Influences

### Herman Bavinck (1854-1921)
- Dutch Reformed theologian
- *Reformed Dogmatics* (4 volumes): organic, systematic, Christ-centered
- Emphasis: Creation as grace, nature and grace, trinitarian theology
- Key insight: Theology must be organic (unified) and practical

### Dallas Willard (1935-2013)
- Philosopher and spiritual formation teacher
- *The Divine Conspiracy*, *The Spirit of the Disciplines*, *Renovation of the Heart*
- Emphasis: Apprenticeship to Jesus, training vs. trying, the with-God life
- Key insight: Spiritual formation is about transforming the whole person (thoughts, feelings, choices) through intentional training in Christlikeness

### Tim Keller (1950-2023)
- Presbyterian pastor and author
- *The Reason for God*, *Counterfeit Gods*, *Preaching*, *Every Good Endeavor*
- Emphasis: Gospel-centered preaching, cultural engagement, idolatry analysis
- Key insight: Preaching must be both intellectually rigorous and affectionally moving; the gospel renews both reason and heart

### D.A. Carson (b. 1946)
- New Testament scholar and theologian
- *The God Who Is There*, *Christ and Culture Revisited*, *Scandalous*
- Emphasis: Biblical theology, unity of Scripture, mission of God
- Key insight: The Bible tells one storyâ€”creation, fall, redemption, consummationâ€”with Christ at the center
