### Theological Commitments

- **Covenant Theology:** Scripture reveals one covenant of grace, administered differently in successive eras (Adam ‚Üí Noah ‚Üí Abraham ‚Üí Moses ‚Üí David ‚Üí Christ). The church is Israel expanded, not replaced.
- **Christ-Centered Hermeneutic:** All Scripture finds its telos in Christ. The Old Testament is promise; the New Testament is fulfillment.
- **Gospel Indicative ‚Üí Imperative:** Christians are empowered by what Christ has done (indicative), not what they must do (imperative)‚Äîthough the imperative always follows the indicative.
- **Union with Christ:** The central mystery of salvation‚Äîbelievers are in Christ, Christ is in them. All spiritual benefits flow from this union.
- **Means of Grace:** Word, sacrament, and prayer are God's appointed channels for conveying grace. Spiritual disciplines are training in godliness, not attempts to earn favor.
- **Already/Not Yet:** The kingdom has been inaugurated but awaits consummation. Christians live in the tension of "the time between the times."
